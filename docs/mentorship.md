# Part 6. Mentorship, expectations, and how we will work together

## 6.1 How we will work

- Regular one-on-ones [proposed cadence, for example weekly 30 to 60 minutes; we will set a standing time]. 
- A simple rule for getting unstuck: try something first, and if you are still stuck after a reasonable effort (say, an afternoon on a technical problem), ask rather than spin. Being stuck is normal and asking early is a strength, not a failing. 
- Default to over-communicating. As you learn the project, flag assumptions and check direction before going deep, it saves rework.
- Use the right channel: quick things in chat, substantive discussion in our one-on-ones or a call, decisions and anything others need to see written down where the team can find it.

## 6.2 What I will provide

- Context and direction, so you understand not just the task but why it matters and how it fits.
- Regular, concrete feedback on your work, delivered directly and kindly.
- Advocacy for your development: connecting you with people, pointing you toward opportunities (conferences, co-authorship, skills), and making sure your growth is part of the plan.
- A reasonable, sustainable pace. This is a technical project and a learning curve is expected; I do not expect you to know it all on arrival, and I would rather you ramp well than burn out.

## 6.3 What I will expect from you

- Ownership of your deliverables.
- Good questions, asked early.
- Reproducible, documented work: organized code and data so that someone else (including future you) can rerun it.
- Engagement with the team and the partners.

## 6.4 Feedback, reviews, and wellbeing

- Feedback runs both ways. I will give you regular feedback, and I want yours. Tell me what is unclear or missing.
- A note on wellbeing: sustainable pace matters, and so does asking for help, including outside work. If something in your workload or circumstances is making things hard, raise it with me early; we can adjust.
