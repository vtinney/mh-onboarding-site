# Onboarding guide: air pollution, health impact assessment, and our projects

---

## How to use this guide

Welcome aboard. This guide is here to help you get up to speed in your first few months. You do not need to read it cover to cover on day one. Here is a sensible path:

1. Use Part 0, practical onboarding, as your week-one setup checklist (accounts, training, logistics). Much of it will be filled in with you.
2. Start with Part 1, the primer. It explains what air pollution is, how it harms health, how we know that, and how greenhouse gases fit into the picture. Read the prose first, then dip into the vetted reading list at the end of Part 1 as time allows.
3. Move to Part 2, on health impact assessment. This is the main method we use. Pay attention to the section that walks from emissions, to atmospheric modeling, to concentrations, to health impacts (there is a diagram), because that pipeline shows up in nearly every project here.
4. Read Part 3, on the projects. The Monterrey project is where most of your time will go, so that section is the most detailed, and it includes a team map and a project timeline. Section 3.6 explains how the science turns into policy.
5. Skim Part 4, the people, and come back to it as you start meeting everyone.
6. Read Part 5 (a suggested first few weeks) and Part 6 (how we will work together) early; they set expectations for the fellowship.

A note on the reading list. Every peer-reviewed article cited in this guide has been checked, so the authors, journal, year, and link are accurate. The list is not meant to be exhaustive or to be read all at once. Treat it as a list to return to, not a syllabus.

A note on style and accuracy. Where this guide gives a number or a specific claim, it comes either from a checked source or from our own project materials. A handful of items are marked "[to confirm]" where a detail (a title, a date, an exact figure) should be verified before it goes into anything external. Please do not propagate those into external materials without checking with me first.
